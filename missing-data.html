Graduate Course Development: Missing Data & Imputation

University of Tennessee, Knoxville · Aug 2024 – Present

Problem

Graduate analytics students often struggle to appropriately diagnose and handle missing data, leading to biased estimates and unreliable models.

My Role

  • Collaborated with BAS faculty to design a graduate-level course module on missing data
  • Developed R functions for missingness diagnostics and multiple imputation (MICE)
  • Researched and integrated alternative imputation techniques and best practices

Tools & Methods

  • R, tidyverse, mice
  • Missing data diagnostics and visualization
  • Multiple imputation validation and data integrity checks

Deliverables

  • Structured course outline and learning objectives
  • Hands-on exercises and example datasets
  • Reusable R functions for instructional use

Impact

Created high-quality instructional material enabling students to correctly diagnose missingness, apply single/multiple imputation, and validate results in applied analytics settings.
