Lake Country DockHounds – Player Scouting & Game-Day Analytics

Baseball Analytics · Scouting & Player Development · 2023 Season

Baseball Analytics Video Data Collection Excel Data Cleaning Reporting Player/Coach Communication Training & Leadership

Problem

The DockHounds needed a repeatable, game-day scouting process to help hitters prepare for opposing starting pitchers. The league had limited data infrastructure (no centralized database or repository), so preparation required building pitch-level insights from scratch using recent appearances and game film—then delivering those insights in a format hitters and coaches could actually use.

My Role

  • Owned the end-to-end game-day scouting workflow for opposing starting pitchers.
  • Created and maintained the structured Excel logging and reporting template.
  • Delivered daily printed reports and communicated key takeaways directly to players/coaches.
  • Trained two interns on pitch identification, data logging, analysis, and presentation for away games.
  • Transitioned the process to an intern lead after leaving for graduate school, ensuring continuity.

Game-Day Workflow (End-to-End)

1) Identify the Starter

  • Each game day began with confirmation from the head coach on the opposing starting pitcher.
  • Located the pitcher’s player profile and last three recorded outings to use as the analysis sample.

2) Baseline Performance Context

  • Used box scores to capture recent performance indicators for hitter context.
  • Logged metrics such as innings pitched, batters faced, strikeouts, walks, pitches thrown, strikes vs balls, runs, earned runs, etc.

3) Video → Structured Pitch Logging

  • Dual-monitor setup: game film on one screen, Excel logging sheet on the other.
  • Captured pitch-level details including:
    • Pitch number (early/late in outing), count (0–0, 3–2, etc.)
    • Pitch type (FB/SL/CB/etc.), velocity (when available), location (outside corner, middle-middle, etc.)
    • Batter handedness (LHB/RHB)
    • Outcome (ball/looking strike/foul/in play + batted-ball result)

4) Clean → Compute → Summarize

  • Cleaned the manually collected data for consistency and accuracy.
  • Computed:
    • Ball-to-strike ratios
    • Pitch mix percentages by batter handedness (LHB vs RHB)
    • Count-based tendencies and location tendencies
  • Translated findings into hitter-ready takeaways and simple visualizations.

5) Deliver & Communicate

  • Printed multiple copies for hitters, coaches, and in-dugout reference.
  • Provided a copy to the color commentator to support in-game storytelling and analysis.
  • For away games, shared the report digitally (e.g., screenshots) via the players’ group chat.

6) Scale the Process (Away Games + Continuity)

  • Worked with two interns during away games—trained them on pitch identification and the full workflow.
  • After returning to school, placed an intern in charge to keep the project running daily.

What Made This Valuable for Hitters

  • Handedness splits: how pitch mix and location changed vs LHB and RHB.
  • Count tendencies: go-to pitches in leverage counts and early-count patterns.
  • Attack plan: concise “what to expect” notes that supported pre-game preparation and in-game adjustments.
  • Simple visuals: designed for fast digestion and dugout usability.

Leadership & Team Enablement

  • Introduced the project under a new coaching staff and scaled it from home games to full-season coverage (home + away).
  • Mentored interns on both the technical work (classification/logging/analysis) and communication (how to present to coaches/players).
  • Created a repeatable process that continued after my departure and remains in use.

Impact

  • Helped establish a consistent hitter-preparation process during a season of organizational transition (new coaching staff).
  • After implementing the daily scouting workflow, the team’s offensive preparation improved and the club narrowly missed the playoffs that year (compared to missing by double digits the year prior).
  • The scouting process continues to be used daily, and the team has made the playoffs in subsequent seasons (2024 and 2025).
